# 🎂 Doña Isabela — Brand Positioning Strategy

A university project where my team applied **Design Thinking** to redesign the brand experience of *Doña Isabela*, a real artisan bakery. The challenge wasn't the product — it was that a brand making genuinely good pastries had a weak identity and almost no emotional connection with its customers.

Instead of just building a website, we designed a full brand-positioning strategy centered on the customer journey, from the very first contact all the way to the after-sale moment.

## 🔍 The Process

We followed the full Design Thinking cycle:

- **Research** — secondary research on emotional storytelling, sensory positioning and food styling, plus a benchmark of bakeries like Sweet & Coffee and Cyrano.
- **Empathize** — interviews, observations, empathy maps and experience maps with the owner, kitchen team, customers and other stakeholders.
- **Define** — we found the real problem wasn't quality. The customer experience felt cold and disconnected because the brand didn't accompany the customer through the journey.
- **Ideate** — many ideas, narrowed down by impact vs. difficulty.
- **Prototype & Validate** — low-fidelity mockups first, then a high-fidelity conceptual web interface, tested with real users.

## 💡 The Insight

> Customers don't just want a good cake. They want a complete experience: personalization, emotion, interaction, and to feel part of the brand.

## ✨ Proposed Solutions

1. **Interactive web gallery** — customers can browse examples, mix products and get inspired before buying.
2. **QR code on packaging** — each order links to behind-the-scenes content showing the artisan process and the story behind the product.
3. **"Photo of the Month" contest** — customers share photos of their orders to join giveaways, generating social content and loyalty.

## ✅ Validation Results

Testing with real users showed the solution was easy to use, made choosing products simpler, created a stronger emotional connection, and motivated social media interaction.

**El proceso:** seguimos el ciclo completo de Design Thinking — investigación secundaria (storytelling emocional, posicionamiento sensorial, food styling y benchmark de Sweet & Coffee y Cyrano), empatía (entrevistas, observaciones, mapas de empatía y de experiencia con la dueña, el equipo de cocina y los clientes), definición, ideación y prototipado/validación.

**El insight:** el verdadero problema no era la calidad. La experiencia del cliente se sentía fría y desconectada porque la marca no lo acompañaba durante todo el proceso de compra.

> El cliente no busca solo un buen pastel. Busca una experiencia completa: personalización, emoción, interacción y sentirse parte de la marca.

**Soluciones propuestas:**
1. **Galería web interactiva** — ver ejemplos, combinar productos e inspirarse antes de comprar.
2. **Código QR en el empaque** — contenido detrás de cámaras sobre el proceso artesanal y la historia del producto.
3. **Concurso "La foto del mes"** — los clientes suben fotos de sus pedidos para participar en sorteos, generando contenido y fidelización.

**Validación:** las pruebas con usuarios mostraron que la solución era fácil de usar, facilitaba elegir productos, generaba mayor conexión emocional y motivaba la interacción en redes.
